# NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF) 2.0

## What is NIST CSF?

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF) is a framework based on cybersecurity best practices that helps organizations manage and reduce cybersecurity risks. Its purpose is to provide a structured approach for identifying critical assets, protecting them, detecting security incidents, responding effectively to cyberattacks, and recovering business operations.

Unlike ISO/IEC 27001, which establishes the requirements for implementing an Information Security Management System (ISMS), NIST CSF provides practical guidance for organizing and improving an organization's cybersecurity capabilities. For this reason, many organizations use both frameworks together, as they complement each other and strengthen the overall security strategy.

## NIST CSF Core Functions

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework is organized into six core functions that help organizations manage cybersecurity risks throughout the entire security lifecycle. These functions provide a structured approach for governing cybersecurity, identifying critical assets, protecting systems, detecting threats, responding to incidents, and recovering business operations.

### Govern

The Govern function establishes the organization's cybersecurity strategy. It defines policies, roles and responsibilities, risk management processes, and ensures that cybersecurity supports business objectives.

### Identify

The Identify function focuses on understanding the organization from a cybersecurity perspective. It involves identifying assets, business processes, critical information, systems, dependencies, and potential risks. This information provides the foundation for assessing cybersecurity risks and prioritizing the protection of the organization's most critical assets.

### Protect

The Protect function focuses on implementing safeguards to reduce cybersecurity risks and protect the organization's critical assets. These safeguards include security policies, access control, security awareness and training, data protection, and technologies that help prevent or minimize the impact of cybersecurity incidents.

### Detect

The Detect function focuses on identifying cybersecurity events and potential security incidents as quickly as possible. It includes continuous monitoring, event analysis, anomaly detection, and processes that help organizations recognize suspicious activities before they cause significant damage.

### Respond

The Respond function focuses on taking appropriate actions after confirming a cybersecurity incident. It includes containing the threat, minimizing its impact, communicating with relevant stakeholders, investigating the incident, and implementing actions to prevent similar incidents in the future.

### Recover

The Recover function focuses on restoring business operations after a cybersecurity incident. It includes recovering systems and data, validating that critical services are operating correctly, communicating the recovery status to stakeholders, and identifying lessons learned to improve the organization's cybersecurity resilience.

## NIST CSF vs ISO/IEC 27001

Although both frameworks aim to improve an organization's security posture, they have different purposes.

| ISO/IEC 27001 | NIST Cybersecurity Framework |
|---------------|------------------------------|
| International standard | Cybersecurity framework |
| Certifiable | Not certifiable |
| Focuses on implementing and maintaining an ISMS | Focuses on managing cybersecurity risk |
| Management system approach | Operational and risk-based approach |
| Defines requirements | Provides best practices and guidance |

Both frameworks are complementary and are frequently implemented together to strengthen an organization's cybersecurity program.

## Real-world Example

A practical approach for implementing cybersecurity is combining ISO/IEC 27001 with the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.

ISO/IEC 27001 provides the requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving an Information Security Management System (ISMS). The NIST Cybersecurity Framework complements this approach by providing practical guidance for governing cybersecurity, identifying critical assets, protecting systems, detecting threats, responding to incidents, and recovering business operations.

Using both frameworks together enables organizations to build a structured security management system while strengthening their operational cybersecurity capabilities.

## Key Takeaways

- NIST CSF is a cybersecurity framework based on best practices.
- It helps organizations manage cybersecurity risks through six core functions.
- The framework is flexible and can be adapted to organizations of any size.
- NIST CSF complements ISO/IEC 27001 rather than replacing it.
- Cybersecurity is a continuous process of governance, identification, protection, detection, response, and recovery.
