# Question 1

## What is the difference between Information Security and Cybersecurity?

### My Answer

Information Security protects all organizational information regardless of its format, whether it is physical, digital, verbal, or stored in the cloud. Its objective is to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

Cybersecurity is a branch of Information Security focused on protecting digital assets such as computers, servers, networks, applications, and electronic information against cyber threats.

### Key Points

- Information Security has a broader scope.
- Cybersecurity focuses on digital assets.
- Cybersecurity is part of Information Security.

# Question 2

## Why is Cybersecurity part of Information Security?

### My Answer

Cybersecurity is part of Information Security because Information Security has a much broader scope. Information Security protects information in any format, while Cybersecurity specifically protects technological environments, digital systems, networks, applications, and electronic information from cyber threats.

Cybersecurity contributes to achieving the objectives of Information Security by protecting digital assets.

### Key Points

- Information Security is broader.
- Cybersecurity protects digital environments.
- Cybersecurity supports Information Security objectives.

# Question 3

## Does having many security controls mean an organization is completely secure?

### My Answer

No.

Implementing security controls such as firewalls, antivirus software, EDR, MFA, and SIEM significantly reduces risk, but it does not eliminate the possibility of a security incident.

Cyber threats, vulnerabilities, and attack techniques evolve continuously. In addition, security depends not only on technology but also on people, processes, risk management, and continuous improvement.

Organizations can become more secure, but they can never be completely immune to cyberattacks.

### Key Points

- Security controls reduce risk.
- Security is not absolute.
- People, processes, and technology are all important.
- Continuous improvement is essential.

# Question 4

## What is the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF) and what is its purpose?

### My Answer

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF) is a cybersecurity framework based on best practices that helps organizations manage cybersecurity risks. Its purpose is to provide a structured approach for governing cybersecurity, identifying critical assets, protecting systems, detecting threats, responding to security incidents, and recovering business operations. It complements ISO/IEC 27001 by strengthening the operational management of cybersecurity.

### Key Points

- Best-practice cybersecurity framework.
- Focused on cybersecurity risk management.
- Organized around six core functions.
- Complements ISO/IEC 27001.

# Question 5

## What are the main differences between ISO/IEC 27001 and NIST CSF?

### My Answer

ISO/IEC 27001 is an international standard that defines the requirements for implementing and maintaining an Information Security Management System (ISMS) and can be certified. NIST CSF is a cybersecurity framework based on best practices that is not certifiable. While ISO/IEC 27001 focuses on establishing a management system, NIST CSF focuses on managing cybersecurity risks through practical operational guidance.

### Key Points

- ISO/IEC 27001 is certifiable.
- NIST CSF is not certifiable.
- ISO focuses on the ISMS.
- NIST focuses on cybersecurity risk management.

# Question 6

## How would you use ISO/IEC 27001 and NIST CSF together in an organization?

### My Answer

I would implement ISO/IEC 27001 to establish and maintain the Information Security Management System (ISMS), including governance, policies, roles, responsibilities, and continuous improvement. At the same time, I would use the NIST Cybersecurity Framework to strengthen cybersecurity operations by identifying critical assets, protecting systems, detecting threats, responding to incidents, and recovering business operations. Together, both frameworks provide a comprehensive and risk-based cybersecurity program.

### Key Points

- ISO establishes the ISMS.
- NIST strengthens cybersecurity operations.
- Both frameworks complement each other.
- Together they improve organizational resilience.
